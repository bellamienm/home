Food around the world has a rich history. The evolution of the human diet has changed vastly over the years. The first humans hunted, fought, and based their whole existence on getting food for their survival. Certain regions grew plants that were indigenous to them and was home to animals that were found nowhere else. As civilization grew and humans evolved, we started cooking and adding flavor to our meals. Various meat and vegetables were added to our diets and spices were discovered for flavor. Our meals have now become a delicacy and all types of regions have come up with recipes that excite our taste buds. Our perception of food has greatly changed from consumption for survival to a fun fest for our tastebuds.

Hamburgers

There is no doubt that hamburgers are loved and enjoyed all over the world, but according to www.therichest.com, the USA consumes the most fast food in the world with burgers taking up more than 50 percent of total fast food expenditure in the country. They have become a favorite go-to meal as they are cheap and easy to eat.

Hamburgers are preferable because the meat is ground up and served in a bun, they invite toppings which add flavor. Because burgers come with an array of sides, you can build yours to your taste. Fast food chains like Mcdonald’s, Burger King, and Subway bring in the most revenue for burger sales in America.

Nachos

The common dish known today as nachos originated in Northern Mexico. The first plate of nachos was made for a group of military officers’ wives in 1943 in Piedras Negras. They had stopped for dinner at The Old Victory Club after a day of shopping where a chef named Anaya put the dish together using leftover ingredients. The plate consisted of plate consisted of tortilla chips, tortilla chips, cheese, and jalapeño peppers.

Nachos are loved for their perfect mix of crunchiness and flavorful cheesy sauces. It has versatile topping options which can suit the cravings of anyone’s palate. Nachos are a type of food that can be mixed up for any occasion because you can change up the toppings to meet the event. A yearly festival called The International Nacho Festival is held in Piedras Negras to celebrate this amazing dish.

Tacos

Tacos are the national dish of Mexico. A taco is anything eaten on a soft tortilla, and there is an infinite variety of them. The word taco dates back to the Mexican silver mines of the 18th century, when it referred to gunpowder that was wrapped in a piece of paper and inserted into rocks. Today, the word is widely known to signify the leading street food and fast food item in Mexico – thin, flat griddle-baked tortillas topped with numerous fillings, folded and eaten without any utensils.

Tacos are thought to come from Mexico long before the Spanish arrived. Ancient Mexicans used freshly made, soft, flat corn tortillas and gave them with fillings like fish and cooked organs. It was a staple meal that provided vital nutrients and energy to those who consumed it. These tacos didn’t contain the cheese, lettuce, sour cream, and tomato that we associate with the meal today. In fact, the taco as we know it is less than 100 years old.

Barbecue

Barbecuing is aloved by Americans because they are not limited to just grilling steak and pork alone. Sausages, seafood, chicken and vegetables are barbecued too, many methods are practiced to give food that smoky barbecue flavour we love. The most famous barbecuing technique enjoyed by Americans is to indirectly grill their meat so it does not cook on an open fire. The barbecue is lit so the fire burns on one side of the grill and food cooks on the unlit side, or the coals are banked on both sides of the grill and the food cooks in the middle.

The American BBQ tradition owes its origins to the fusion of many cultural food traditions, including African and Caribbean.At its earliest origins, barbecue involved slow-cooking meat over sticks and saplings, a common activity among indigenous Americans and Caribbeans. Today barbecuing has become a form of enjoyment as meat, usually pork, is put over a charcoal grill and covered in spices and herbs. Americans love cooking outdoors because it improves the meat’s flavor, for personal enjoyment and for entertaining family and friends.

Mac and Cheese

Mac and cheese - formerly known as macaroni and cheese - is a famous dish in America for its cheesy goodness and comes in many delicious variations. The main ingredients are macaroni, cheese, and butter. It is prepared by cooking pasta and a cheesy white sauce in separate pans, then combining it to bake in an oven with even more cheese.

The US president Thomas Jefferson encountered mac and cheese in Paris, the dish soon became popularized with the help of his slave who was a French chef. Mac and cheese is loved by Americans for how quick and easy is it to prepare, making it a go-to dish for anyone who wants a filling meal but doesn’t have the best culinary skills.

Stew

It is one of the most popular delicacies loved by meat lovers, for many countries, it is a popular comfort food. Putting a date on the world’s first bowl of stew is probably impossible. Anthropologists haven’t been able to determine for certain when man was first able to control fire, or when cooking itself was invented. Through much of human history, stews were much simpler, requiring nothing more than adding a chunk of meat or other foods to boiling water to make a nourishing broth. Stews are believed to have been prepared in waterproof and heatproof containers found in 20,000-year-old caves in China by Harvard University archeologists, Ofer Bar-Yosef. Stews could also have been cooked by Neanderthals, by dropping heated rocks into a pit lined with animal skin, which could heat water and other ingredients.

Pancakes

Pancakes are a staple breakfast in many American households, they have been around for 30,000 years (since the stone age). Researchers have even found wheat-based pancakes in the stomach remains of the mummified 5,000 year old body of Ötzi The Iceman. Greeks and Romans made this breakfast treat using wheat flour, olive oil, honey, and curdled milk, which is a little different from the pancakes we know today. As time passed, people started flavoring their pancakes using rose water, spices, sherry, and apples. However, while the first reference to pancakes came from ancient Greece, variations of pancakes existed in most countries around the world for centuries in some form as a breakfast food or treat similar to, but sweeter than, bread.

Bread

According to history, the earliest bread was made in or around 8000 BC in the Middle East, specifically Egypt. The quern was the first known grinding tool. Grain was crushed and the bakers produced what we now commonly recognize in its closest form as chapatis (India) or tortillas (Mexico). It is thought that their brewing expertise combined with the warm climate produced the world’s first sourdough . Apparently, the use of fermentation to make bread rise was discovered by accident, when someone left some dough in the sun and it rose. Since then, bread achieved distinction as a staple food product in all parts of the world. In the following centuries, countries developed their versions of bread. Some leavened, others not.
